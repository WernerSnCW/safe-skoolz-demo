## SECTION 01 — MISSION AND VISION

**Mission statement**
SafeSchool exists to make every school a provably safe place — where harm is identified early, reported honestly, and acted on transparently.

**Winning aspiration**
We have won when a headteacher in any British curriculum school, anywhere in the world, can demonstrate to parents, governors, and regulators that their safeguarding system works — not because they say so, but because the data shows it.

**What winning is not**
- Winning is not becoming a general school management platform. We do not compete with SIMS, iSAMS, or Arbor.
- Winning is not the cheapest incident logging tool. We compete on compliance intelligence and parental trust, not on price.
- Winning is not UK-only. British curriculum schools operate in 160+ countries. The product must be portable.

## SECTION 02 — VALUES

**Primary values**

1. **The child is the client.** Every feature, every report, every data point serves child safety first. When commercial incentives and child outcomes conflict, child outcomes win. This is why we do not sell to schools that want a system to manage optics. We sell to schools that want to genuinely improve.

2. **Transparency is the product.** We do not help schools hide incidents. The entire value proposition depends on honest, auditable records. A school that uses SafeSchool to suppress information is a school we walk away from.

3. **Parents deserve to know.** Our parent visibility module is a core feature, not an optional add-on. This is a decision that costs us commercial ease with some schools — and we make it deliberately.

4. **Regulation is an opportunity, not a burden.** LOPIVI in Spain, Keeping Children Safe in Education in the UK, and equivalent frameworks in 160+ countries are not compliance headaches. They are the structural argument for why every school needs this product. We build to those frameworks explicitly.

## SECTION 04 — MARKET AND GEOGRAPHY

**Primary geography**
United Kingdom — and British curriculum schools operating internationally. This is a global market served through a common curriculum, regulatory, and language framework. The UK anchor gives us KCSIE compliance as a baseline. International expansion follows the British Schools Overseas (BSO) and Council of British International Schools (COBIS) networks.

**Target segment definition**
Primary: British curriculum independent and international schools, 200–2,000 pupils. Secondary (Phase 2): state-maintained schools in England and Wales. Tertiary (Phase 3): non-British international schools in LOPIVI-regulated jurisdictions (Spain priority).

**Segment size / proxy**
- ~2,600 independent schools in the UK (ISC member schools), collectively educating ~550,000 pupils
- ~5,500 British curriculum international schools globally (British Council estimate)
- Spain alone has ~500 international schools, of which ~180 operate on British or IB curriculum
- Combined addressable market: 8,000+ schools at target segment definition

**Why now**
Three forces are converging simultaneously:
1. **Regulatory tightening.** KCSIE 2023/24 updates introduced explicit requirements for record-keeping, pattern detection, and multi-agency information sharing. Schools that were managing safeguarding informally are now exposed. The September 2024 KCSIE revision increased scrutiny of online safety and contextual safeguarding — areas where paper systems fail entirely.
2. **Parental expectation shift.** Post-pandemic, parents at fee-paying schools expect institutional transparency. The market for premium safeguarding assurance is being created by parents who have already seen institutions fail their children and who are unwilling to accept opacity.
3. **Competitor gap.** MyConcern (from £429/year) and CPOMS (undisclosed pricing) are the dominant tools. Both were built for constrained UK state school budgets. Neither has LOPIVI compliance, pupil-facing emotional reporting, or regulatory export functions. Neither offers parental visibility. The international school segment is entirely unserved by purpose-built tools.

**Expansion roadmap**
- Phase 1 (2025–2026): UK independent schools and Spanish international schools. Pilot at Morna International College cohort.
- Phase 2 (2027–2028): British curriculum international schools across Europe and Middle East.
- Phase 3 (2029+): Broader international school market. Partnership with COBIS and FOBISIA networks.

## SECTION 05 — CORE NARRATIVE

**Problem statement**
School safeguarding systems fail children because they were not designed to protect children — they were designed to protect schools. Incidents are logged in isolation. Patterns go undetected. Reports are compiled and withheld. Parents are told what the school decides they need to know. When a child is harmed repeatedly, the system produces paperwork, not protection.

The structural failure is not malice — it is architecture. Generic incident-logging tools were built for budget-constrained state schools focused on compliance box-ticking, not for fee-paying schools where parents expect genuine accountability. The result: a £20,000-per-year school operating a safeguarding system less sophisticated than a spreadsheet.

**Solution statement**
SafeSchool replaces the fragmented, opaque, paper-and-email system with a platform built around three mechanisms that do not exist anywhere else in this market:
1. **Pattern detection** — the system identifies when a child is involved in multiple incidents over time, even when each incident is handled by a different member of staff
2. **Regulatory-grade reporting** — every incident is recorded in a format that maps directly to KCSIE, LOPIVI, and equivalent frameworks, producing audit-ready evidence rather than internal notes
3. **Parental visibility** — parents receive structured, appropriate updates on concerns involving their child, replacing the current system of selective disclosure by the school

**Brand story**
SafeSchool was founded by parents who experienced directly what happens when a prestigious international school's safeguarding system fails. Their daughter was subjected to sustained bullying and sexual harassment. The school logged incidents in isolation. Patterns were invisible. A formal safeguarding report was compiled and withheld from the family for days. There was no school counsellor for eight months.

After documenting this experience formally — including a recorded emergency PTA meeting in which the headteacher confirmed on the record that incidents had been treated in isolation and Year 5 parents had not been notified — the founders did not just pursue the school. They built the solution.

Every feature in SafeSchool exists because a real school failed to have it. That is a founding story no competitor can replicate.

**StoryBrand one-liner**
When schools can't see the full picture, children pay the price. SafeSchool gives every school the pattern detection, audit trail, and parental transparency they need to protect every child — and prove it.

**What makes this defensible**
- Founding story creates authentic authority in a market where trust is everything
- Regulatory alignment (KCSIE + LOPIVI + international equivalents) creates switching costs — once a school's safeguarding history lives in SafeSchool, migration risk is high
- Parent visibility module creates a constituency (parents) who will demand it — pulling the product into schools rather than requiring pure top-down sales
- International school market is structurally unserved — no competitor has built for this segment

## SECTION 06 — TARGET AUDIENCE AND PERSONAS

**Audience summary**
SafeSchool serves three decision-making stakeholders at fee-paying British curriculum schools: the head or principal (accountable for compliance), the designated safeguarding lead (DSL, operationally responsible), and parents (the accountability constituency who create demand). Sales motions run through the head and DSL; demand is often created by parents.

**Persona 1: The Accountable Head**
- **Motivation:** Avoid regulatory, reputational, and legal exposure. Demonstrate institutional quality to governors and prospective parents.
- **Behaviours:** Delegates operational safeguarding to DSL. Engages when there is a complaint, inspection threat, or governance challenge.
- **Pain points:** Cannot get a clear picture of the school's safeguarding health. Relies on DSL's verbal updates. Has no audit trail that would withstand an Ofsted or equivalent inspection.
- **Platform use:** Review dashboard. Export compliance reports. Respond to governor or parent escalations.
- **Key language:** "We take safeguarding very seriously." "We follow all required procedures." "I have full confidence in our DSL."
- **Approximate share of buying decisions:** 70% — the head typically authorises spend.
- **Lifecycle stage:** Engaged when compliance risk is visible. Ideal entry point is post-incident, post-inspection warning, or peer-school embarrassment.

**Persona 2: The Overwhelmed DSL**
- **Motivation:** Do the job properly. Not be the person holding the liability when something goes wrong.
- **Behaviours:** Manages safeguarding operationally using a mix of paper files, email threads, and generic school MIS. Knows the current system is inadequate. Lacks the authority to procure new tools alone.
- **Pain points:** Cannot see patterns across incidents. Spends hours producing reports from fragmented sources. Dreads inspections because the evidence base is weak.
- **Platform use:** Daily incident logging. Pattern alerts. Report generation for external agencies.
- **Key language:** "I just don't have visibility across everything." "If there was ever an inquiry I'd be the one holding incomplete records." "I know more happens than gets reported to me."
- **Approximate share of buying decisions:** 30% authority — but 100% influence. DSL advocacy is required for any sale to close.
- **Lifecycle stage:** Ready to champion the product. Needs to be equipped to make the internal case to the head.

**Persona 3: The Demanding Parent**
- **Motivation:** Know their child is safe. Trust that the school will act, not manage.
- **Behaviours:** Pays premium fees with an expectation of premium accountability. Engaged with the PTA. Vocal when expectations are not met.
- **Pain points:** No visibility into what the school does when a concern is raised. Receives selective information. Has no way to verify that the school is acting.
- **Platform use:** Parent-facing visibility module — structured updates on concerns involving their child.
- **Key language:** "I just want to know what's actually happening." "I don't want to find out something was dealt with badly after the fact." "The school says they handled it but I have no way to verify that."
- **Approximate share of buying decisions:** 0% direct. High indirect influence — organised parent groups create reputational and governance pressure that moves heads to act.
- **Lifecycle stage:** Creates the burning platform that makes the head engage.

## SECTION 07 — VALUE PROPOSITION

**Core value proposition**
SafeSchool is the only platform built specifically for British curriculum schools that makes safeguarding auditable, pattern-aware, and transparent to parents — satisfying the school's regulatory obligations and the parent's expectation of accountability simultaneously.

**Value by persona**

| Persona | The core value delivered |
|---|---|
| The Accountable Head | A defensible audit trail. If a safeguarding failure is ever alleged, the school can demonstrate that every incident was recorded, every pattern was surfaced, and every parent was appropriately informed. |
| The Overwhelmed DSL | A system that does the pattern detection and report generation they currently do by hand — faster, more reliably, and in a format regulators will accept. |
| The Demanding Parent | Real-time visibility into how concerns involving their child are being handled — replacing selective verbal updates with a structured, timestamped record. |

**Independence / differentiation claim**
SafeSchool is not affiliated with any school group, academy trust, or local authority. We do not have a commercial interest in how a school's safeguarding record looks. Our only commercial interest is that the system works — because a school that can prove its safeguarding works renews its contract, and a school that has something to hide eventually becomes a liability.

**Why no incumbent can replicate this**
MyConcern and CPOMS were built for UK state schools managing tight budgets and minimal parental engagement. Their architecture reflects that: low-cost, single-stakeholder (DSL-focused), compliance-checkbox tools. Rebuilding them for international, multi-stakeholder, regulatory-multi-jurisdiction use would require a ground-up rewrite. They will not do this — the state school market is their core revenue base and they have no incentive to cannibalise it with a premium international product.
