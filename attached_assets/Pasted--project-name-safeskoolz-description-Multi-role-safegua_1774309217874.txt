Thought for 23s
This is a seriously solid spec. It already looks like a real product brief, not a vague startup napkin.
My read: the concept is strong, the role model is rich, and the architecture is sensible. The main work now is not “more features,” it’s tightening the risky edges so it is actually deployable in a school setting like Morna.

Overall verdict
Yes — this is viable as a pilotable platform.
The strongest bits are:
very clear role separation
proper multi-tenant school model
good incident/protocol split
strong PTA layer without giving PTA direct access to child-level data
diary privacy being protected from staff/parent access
pattern detection being framed as alerts, not conclusions
decent monorepo structure for long-term maintainability
The bit I’d focus on now is this:
Turn it from a feature-rich school app into a defensible safeguarding system of record.
That means tightening the compliance model, access control, audit behaviour, and a few naming/workflow issues.
What looks especially good
The best design decision in here is the separation between:
incident
protocol
pattern alert
audit log
That is exactly the right direction.
Why it matters:

an incident is a reported event
a protocol is the formal institutional response
a pattern alert is an internal signal
an audit log is the legal/accountability trail
That separation will save you massive pain later.
Also, your compliance framing is on the right track. LOPIVI requires schools with minors to have a coordinator/coordinadora de bienestar y protección under the supervision of the head or owner, and the Balearic Convivèxit materials set out formal bullying case workflows and annexes, including the revised school bullying protocol from May 2024. CAIB also publishes a separate protocol and annex structure for violències masclistes in educational settings. 

That means your idea of:

framework assignment
annex template library
referral body directory
formal protocol opening and management
is exactly the right product direction. 
The biggest issues I’d fix before build or pilot
1. “Teacher consent” is misnamed and slightly muddled
You describe:
coordinator requests consent
parent approves/declines
teacher sees redacted/unredacted view depending on consent
That is not “teacher consent.” That is really:
parental disclosure consent
or parental release permission
Right now the field names will confuse everybody, including developers and users.
Better naming:

disclosure_consent_status
disclosure_consent_requested_at
disclosure_consent_responded_at
disclosure_scope
Even better: define what consent governs:
teacher sees full narrative?
names of involved pupils?
only safeguarding recommendation?
only class action required?
That needs to be explicit.
2. Public pupil listing is too risky
This endpoint is a red flag:
GET /api/schools/:schoolId/pupils — public, minimal data
Even with “minimal data,” this creates a pupil enumeration risk.
Safer options:

require school selection + class/year + PIN pre-step
show avatar choices only after a valid school-specific code
use a login token / QR / roster code for pupil login flows
never publicly expose a raw list of pupils by school ID
I would strongly change this before launch.
3. 4-digit pupil PIN is okay for demo, weak for production
A 4-digit PIN is only 10,000 combinations. Even with rate limiting and lockout, that is flimsy if pupils can be enumerated.
Better production approach:

4-digit PIN plus avatar confirmation or birth-month choice
school-scoped login code
or 6-digit PIN for production tenants
Also: 90 seconds inactivity for pupils feels too aggressive. Good safeguarding instinct, but in practice it may create distress/friction for a child writing a diary or reporting something difficult.
Better:

3–5 minutes inactivity
with immediate blur/lock on tab switch or explicit “quick hide” button
4. Immutable audit log vs data protection rights
“Immutable audit logs” are good, but you need a careful legal/data model, because blanket immutability can clash with rectification/retention obligations.
The right answer is usually:

audit entries are append-only
corrections are new compensating entries
personal data in logs is minimised
retention policy is explicit by record type
redaction is policy-driven, not destructive editing
So: good principle, but it needs a retention + minimisation policy baked into the schema and product logic.
5. Diary privacy promise is excellent — but the AI scan changes the privacy story
You say:
“Only the pupil can see their entries — no parent, teacher, or coordinator access”
That is great. But then:
“AI safeguarding scan after each entry”
“Flagged entries create pattern_alert”
That means the diary is not truly “only the pupil can see it.”
A machine is processing it, and the system may act on it.
That is still workable, but the product language must be precise:

Recommended wording:

“Diary entries are not viewable by parents, teachers, or coordinators.”
“Entries are automatically scanned for serious safeguarding risk.”
“If risk is detected, the system creates a safeguarding alert without exposing the diary text.”
That is a much cleaner and safer promise.
6. PTA anonymised analytics need minimum-threshold suppression
PTA access is a clever feature, but anonymisation can fail fast in small schools.
Example:

Year 6 has 7 pupils
one sexual incident
one SEN-related case
one obvious timing clue
Everyone works out who it is.
You need hard rules like:

no subgroup chart if population < 10
no category displayed if count < 3
no timeframe slicing that reveals individual cases
noise/suppression for very small schools
Otherwise the PTA layer becomes a re-identification machine.
7. Role overlap needs a permission matrix, not just role descriptions
You have a good role catalogue, but for build quality you now need a proper matrix:
For each endpoint/action define:

approve

Especially important for:

Because “same as coordinator” is fine in the brief, but dangerous in code.
8. The platform needs explicit “mandatory action” rules, not just “mandatory referral engine”
This is a big one.
You mention a referral engine, which is good. But the system should explicitly distinguish:

alert
internal action required
protocol must be opened
external referral required
leadership notification required
Why? Because official school protocols are procedural, not just analytical. The Convivèxit bullying materials and the Balearic protocol structure are annex/workflow-heavy, not just “spot a pattern and show a dashboard.”

That will make the app feel institutional, not cosmetic.
