MORNA INTERNATIONAL COLLEGE
Zero Tolerance Policy on Bullying, Harassment and Sexual Misconduct
Proposed by the Parent Teacher Association · March 2026



Policy framework
This policy sits under the school's Safeguarding and Child Protection Policy and operates in accordance with LOPIVI (Ley Orgánica 8/2021), the Balearic Islands Convivèxit bullying protocol, the Balearic machista violence protocol (2022), and Morna's own anti-bullying framework. In the event of any conflict, the applicable Spanish legal framework takes precedence.
Scope: This policy applies to all pupils, staff and visitors. It covers behaviour on school premises, online conduct, and school-related off-site behaviour, including trips and events.


1. What "zero tolerance" means
Zero tolerance does not mean automatic expulsion for every incident. It means that no incident of bullying, harassment or sexual misconduct is ignored, minimised or treated as acceptable. Every incident must be recorded, assessed and met with a defined and proportionate response. The question is not whether the school will respond — it is how.

2. Behaviour categories and mandatory responses
The examples given in each category are illustrative and not exhaustive. The school must apply the category that most accurately reflects the conduct, including using more precise legal terminology where it applies.



Category 1 — Low-level single incident
Examples (illustrative, not exhaustive): one-off unkind comment, minor exclusion, brief argument.
Mandatory response:
Conversation with the children involved
Incident logged with date, names and description
Behaviour monitored for repetition
Parents informed if behaviour continues
No formal sanction is required at this stage.




Category 2 — Repeated or deliberate targeting
Examples (illustrative, not exhaustive): repeated name-calling, deliberate exclusion, property damage, relational manipulation, coercive direction of other children.
Mandatory response:
Formal incident log opened
Parents of all children involved notified in writing within 48 hours
Formal warning issued
Behaviour monitoring plan put in place
Follow-up with the victim within one week




Category 3 — Serious misconduct
Examples (illustrative, not exhaustive): physical assault, sustained targeted bullying, coercive use of other children, coordinated group targeting.
Mandatory response:
Formal safeguarding protocol opened immediately
Designated coordinator assumes case management
Separate interviews conducted with victim, perpetrators and witnesses
Internal suspension of at least 3 school days
Parents of all children formally notified the same day
Written risk assessment completed before return to shared spaces
Behaviour contract issued on return
If behaviour continues or the behaviour contract is breached, fixed-term exclusion will follow.




Category 4 — Immediate safeguarding: sexual harassment or sexual violence
Examples (illustrative, not exhaustive): sexualised comments including so-called "banter", unwanted touching, attempting to kiss without consent, surrounding or intimidating a child with sexual intent, threats of a sexual nature. These obligations apply regardless of the age of the children involved.
Mandatory response:
Immediate separation of the children involved
Designated coordinator and headteacher notified within one hour
Formal protocol opened under the Balearic machista violence framework
Parents of the victim notified the same day with the protocol reference number and coordinator's name
Parents of the perpetrators notified the same day in writing
Internal suspension of at least 5 school days
External assessment of perpetrators required, unless the headteacher records in writing why that step is not necessary in the circumstances
Behaviour contract with an express zero-tolerance clause required before return
Where there is any recurrence, fixed-term exclusion will apply. Permanent exclusion will be considered where there is an established pattern, repeated conduct, or conduct of sufficient seriousness, in accordance with applicable Spanish education law and the school's exclusion procedure. Referral to the Fiscalía de Menores will be considered where the conduct may amount to a criminal offence.


3. Escalation ladder
The following steps apply across all categories. Escalation is mandatory where lower steps have not produced the required change in behaviour.

Step 1 — Verbal warning: Logged. Parents informed. One-week monitoring period.
Step 2 — Formal written warning: Logged. Parents called to a meeting. Written record sent home. Behaviour monitoring plan activated.
Step 3 — Internal suspension: Pupil removed from class and shared spaces. Structured reflection work set. Parents formally notified in writing. Return conditional on a meeting with the coordinator and parents.
Step 4 — Behaviour contract: Written agreement signed by pupil, parents and school. Specific behaviours identified. Clear consequences for breach stated. Review date set. Any breach automatically triggers Step 5.
Step 5 — Fixed-term exclusion: Formal exclusion for a defined period. Re-entry meeting required. Behaviour contract reviewed and updated. A second fixed-term exclusion triggers consideration of Step 6.
Step 6 — Permanent exclusion: Applied where conduct is sufficiently serious, Category 4 behaviour is repeated, a behaviour contract has been breached, alternative measures have been exhausted, or the safety of other pupils cannot otherwise be assured. Any permanent exclusion process must comply with applicable Spanish legal requirements.

4. Non-negotiable principles



Separation first
In any Category 3 or Category 4 incident, the children involved must be separated before any other response is taken. This is mandatory.




No return to shared spaces until risk assessed
A child who has been subjected to a serious incident must not be required to return to shared classes or spaces with the perpetrator until a written risk assessment has been completed and protective measures are in place.




Cumulative pattern assessment
Every incident must be assessed in light of previous incidents involving the same children. Three or more incidents involving the same victim and perpetrator in one term automatically trigger a formal protocol review, regardless of the individual seriousness of each incident viewed in isolation.




No minimising language
Incidents must be recorded using the most accurate and precise language available under the applicable framework, including terms such as sexual harassment, sustained targeted bullying, and coercive behaviour where appropriate. Vague expressions such as "inappropriate behaviour" or "falling out" must not be used where a more precise categorisation applies.




Consistent consequences
The same conduct must attract the same category of response regardless of the identity, status or history of the pupil involved.




Victim protection
Where a child reacts to sustained bullying, harassment or targeting, that reaction must be considered in its full context. A victim must not be sanctioned without proper consideration of the harm they have been experiencing and whether their response was a reaction to that harm. Where a victim is identified, the school must offer support measures including welfare check-ins, counselling referral and adjusted seating or timetabling arrangements as appropriate.



5. Annual review
This policy will be reviewed annually by the school in consultation with the PTA. The review will consider pupil, parent and staff feedback and current legal and regulatory guidance. An anonymised annual report will be provided to parents setting out:
the number of formal protocols opened
the categories of behaviour recorded
the outcomes reached
any changes made to the policy or its implementation
